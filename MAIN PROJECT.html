PATH BREAKING DISCOVERIES OF 21ST CENTURY



THE 2010S come to an end, we can look back on an era rife with discovery. In the past 10 years, scientists around the world made remarkable progress toward understanding the human body, our planet, and the cosmos that surrounds us. What’s more, science in the 2010s became more global and collaborative than ever before. These days, major breakthroughs are likelier to come from groups of 3,000 scientists than groups of three. So much has happened, thanks to so many, that National Geographic’s writers and editors decided not to whittle down the last decade into just a handful of discoveries. Instead, we’ve put our heads together to identify 5 trends and milestones that we found especially noteworthy, and that we think will set the stage for more amazing finds in the decade to come.


SPACE


DETECTION OF GRAVITATIONAL WAVES

LARGEST BLACK HOLE EVER DISCOVERED

The existence of gravitational waves was first predicted by Albert Einstein in 1916. And finally, they got detected at LIGO in year 2016(Exactly after 100 years of their prediction by Einstein!)

Astronomers have found the biggest black hole ever measured — it's 40 billion times the sun’s mass, or roughly two-thirds the mass of all stars in the Milky Way.


SCIENCE


TRACKING DOWN THE GOD PARTICLE

DISCOVERY OF QUARK-GLUON PLASMA

How does matter get mass? In the 1960s and 1970s, physicists including Peter Higgs and François Englert proposed a solution in the form of a novel energy field that permeates the universe, now called the Higgs field.

Quark–gluon plasma filled the entire Universe before matter was created. Theories predicting the existence of quark–gluon plasma were developed in the late 1970s and early 1980s.Quark–gluon plasma was detected for the first time in the laboratory at CERN in the year 2000.


TECH


COMPUTER CHIPS THAT CAN REPLICATE HUMAN BRAIN

Artificial neural networks, computer algorithms that take inspiration from the human brain, have demonstrated fancy feats such as detecting lies, recognizing faces, and predicting heart attacks.
